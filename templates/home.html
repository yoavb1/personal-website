About Me

I'm Yoav Ben Yaakov, a Ph.D. candidate at the School of Industrial & Intelligent Systems Engineering, Tel Aviv University, Israel.

I'm currently seeking a postdoctoral position in the areas of human-AI interaction Human Factors, or behavioral modeling. If you're hiring or know of relevant opportunities, feel free to get in touch.

My research lies at the intersection of Human Factors and Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), with a particular focus on how people interact with Decision Support (DS) and AI Systems in uncertain environments.

During my doctoral studies, I explore how system attributes, such as reliability, cost, and display format, along with contextual factors like uncertainty and information redundancy, influence the decision to engage with automation.

My work examines how these factors affect user trust, task performance, decision latency, and cognitive workload, and how humans incorporate automated input into their own judgments.

I use behavioral experiments, cognitive modeling, and quantitative analysis to investigate where and why human decisions diverge from normative models. Ultimately, my goal is to inform the design of intelligent systems that are not only powerful, but also aligned with human cognitive strategies and limitations.

Additional Research Interests

Beyond my doctoral research, I am also exploring how users interact with AI-powered conversational systems, particularly large language model (LLM) chatbots. This project examines how interface features, such as display format (word-by-word vs. full message) and access to conversation history, shape users' willingness to engage with chatbots, their trust in the information provided, and their cognitive workload.
